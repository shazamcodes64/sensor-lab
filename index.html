🖥️ LCD
WHAT IS AN LCD?

An LCD uses liquid crystals — molecules that twist or untwist when voltage is applied — to control whether light passes through or gets blocked.

Sandwiched between two polarizing filters. Voltage OFF → crystals aligned → light passes → bright. Voltage ON → crystals untwist → light blocked → dark.

⚡ WOW FACT

Every pixel on your phone does this ON/OFF switch thousands of times per second to form the image you see.

KEY CONCEPTS
  • Combination of solid & liquid states of matter
  • Works by blocking light, not emitting it
  • Uses polarizing filters + liquid crystal layer
  • Electrode made of indium-tin oxide (ITO)
  • Thinner & lighter than CRT displays
  • Consumes microwatts vs milliwatts for LED
PROS & CONS

✓ PROS

Low power · Thin & light · Low cost · Great contrast

✗ CONS

Needs backlight · Limited temp · Slow response · Needs AC

📡 PROXIMITY
WHAT IS A PROXIMITY SENSOR?

Detects nearby objects without physical contact by emitting an electromagnetic field or radiation and watching for changes in the return signal.

⚡ WOW FACT

Your phone turns off its screen the instant you lift it to your ear — a proximity sensor does this in under 10 milliseconds.

TYPES

INDUCTIVE

Detects metal via eddy current losses in a magnetic field

CAPACITIVE

Detects any material via capacitance change

MAGNETIC

Reed contacts activated by a permanent magnet

PHOTOELECTRIC

Light beam interruption detection

REAL-WORLD USES
  • 📱 Phone screen off near ear
  • 🚰 Automatic faucets
  • 🚗 Car parking sensors
  • ✈️ Aircraft ground proximity warning
  • 🎢 Roller coasters & conveyor systems

🔴 IR SENSOR
WHAT IS AN IR SENSOR?

An electronic device that emits and/or detects infrared radiation — light beyond the red end of the visible spectrum that human eyes cannot see.

⚡ WOW FACT

Your TV remote fires invisible IR pulses at 38,000 Hz. Point it at your phone camera — you'll see it flash!

ACTIVE vs PASSIVE

ACTIVE IR

LED emitter + receiver. Detects objects by reflection. Used in robots.

PASSIVE IR (PIR)

Only detects IR. Uses pyroelectric material. Detects body heat.

HOW IT WORKS
  • Step 1 LED emitter fires infrared beam
  • Step 2 Beam travels invisibly through air
  • Step 3 Beam reflects off target object
  • Step 4 Receiver photodiode detects reflection
  • Step 5 Signal processed → action triggered
